ExtraDisplay
Download How to use it

Use Tesla touch screen as an extended wireless monitor for your MacBook.

Download for Mac

Compatible with any car or tablet with a modern web browser. Windows app is under development.

How to use it

This app works when your Mac and Tesla browser can reach each other on the same Wi-Fi local network.

Method 1: USB iPhone Tethering

This is the easiest and recommended option.

  1. Connect the iPhone to the Mac with a USB data cable
  2. On the iPhone: Turn on Settings > Personal Hotspot
  3. On the Mac: Go to Settings > Sharing > Internet Sharing
    Pick "iPhone" Under "Share your connection from"
    Toggle on "Wi-Fi" under "To Device Using"
    Use simple names and avoid spaces when naming the network, for example "macbook"
    Pick "WPA2/WPA3 Personal" under Security and set a password for this Wi-Fi signal
  4. Join the "macbook" Wi-Fi network from the Tesla car Settings > Wi-Fi
  5. Launch ExtraDisplay app on your Mac, click "Start"
  6. Visit the address from the Tesla web browser and click "Connect"
  7. When prompted, allow the app under Privacy & Security > Recording
    if "Enable touch" is checked in the app, Privacy & Security > Accessiblity permission will be requested

Method 2: Portable Wi-Fi Router

Wi-Fi Router knowledge required.

  1. Read this instruction to setup the portable Wi-Fi router first
  2. On the iPhone: Turn on Settings > Personal Hotspot
  3. Connect the iPhone to the router with a USB data cable
  4. Join the router Wi-Fi network from the MacBook and the Tesla car
  5. Launch ExtraDisplay app on your Mac, click "Start"
  6. Visit the address from the Tesla web browser and click "Connect"
  7. When prompted, allow the app under Privacy & Security > Recording
    if "Enable touch" is checked in the app, Privacy & Security > Accessiblity permission will be requested
